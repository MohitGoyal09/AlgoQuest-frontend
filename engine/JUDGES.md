## Judge Profiles

### 1. Nilabh Kumar -- VP (Strategy & Operations), Gartner

**Company:** Gartner is the global authority on technology markets. They publish Magic Quadrants, Hype Cycles, and market forecasts that determine what enterprises buy. 20,000+ employees globally, major India GCC in Gurgaon.

**Role:** Strategy & Operations VP. Manages P&L, resource allocation, cross-functional execution. Business-focused with deep analytical skills. Understands tech markets from an advisory lens, not engineering.

**Industry context:** Gartner employees are 100% knowledge workers -- analysts, consultants, salespeople. All digital. But Nilabh evaluates products across ALL industries for clients.

**What he knows:** Every competitor in the employee experience space. Culture Amp, Lattice, Viva Insights, Peakon, Qualtrics. He can name ten more you have not heard of.

**What he will ask:** "What Gartner market category does this fall into? What is your competitive moat against Qualtrics EX and Workday Peakon?"

**How to impress:** Speak Gartner language. "We are creating a new category -- metadata-only behavioral signal intelligence. Not Voice of Employee, not performance management. Proactive workforce risk detection." He loves category creators.

**What NOT to say:** Do not claim to replace Culture Amp or Lattice. Position as complementary. Do not be vague about where you sit in the market -- Gartner people hate products that cannot articulate their category.

### 2. Dr. Veni Mathur -- Vice Chairperson, CILT India

**Company:** CILT (Chartered Institute of Logistics & Transport) is a professional body for logistics and transport professionals. Provides internationally recognized qualifications. Focuses on railways, roads, waterways, supply chain.

**Role:** Transport economist with a Ph.D. from IIT Delhi. Visiting faculty at IIT Delhi for 15+ years. Head of Education and Training Committee at CILT India. Expertise in Indian Railways cost analysis, transport economics, logistics policy.

**Industry context:** CILT's world is physical -- trucks, trains, ships, warehouses. Workers are drivers, dock staff, warehouse operators. Zero digital footprint in Slack or GitHub. She thinks in macro-economics and cost-benefit analysis.

**What she knows:** Indian labor markets, workforce economics, informal economy dynamics, government employment schemes (ESI, PMJAY).

**What she will ask:** "How does this apply to transport workers who don't use computers? What is the ROI calculation? What about India's informal labor force?"

**How to impress:** Use economic language. "Employee burnout costs Indian enterprises INR 1.2 lakh crore annually in attrition and productivity loss. Sentinel reduces this by providing early warning signals, cutting replacement costs by 40-60%." Cite research.

**What NOT to say:** Do not use SaaS jargon (ARR, MRR, churn). She does not think in startup metrics. Speak in economic impact and policy terms.

### 3. Dr. Chetana Chaudhuri -- Fellow, NCAER

**Company:** NCAER is India's oldest and largest independent economic policy research think tank, established 1956. Publishes studies that influence Indian government policy. About 100-200 researchers and staff.

**Role:** Senior Fellow specializing in CGE Modelling, Energy Economics, Employment and Health Economics. Ph.D. from JNU. Previously at NIPFP, Public Health Foundation of India. She evaluates government schemes for effectiveness.

**Industry context:** Academic/research institution. Government-adjacent. She thinks in terms of evidence, methodology, statistical significance, and policy impact.

**What she knows:** Indian labor market data, health economics research, benefit-cost analysis frameworks, how government schemes fail.

**What she will ask:** "Where is the peer-reviewed validation? What is the false positive rate? How does this address blue-collar and gig workers? Can this data feed into policy-making?"

**How to impress:** Lead with methodology. "Our detection uses linear regression for velocity, Shannon entropy for schedule chaos, and graph centrality for network analysis -- all established, peer-reviewed mathematical methods. The thresholds are our hypothesis, which we plan to validate through a 6-month shadow deployment."

**What NOT to say:** Do not make unvalidated claims about prediction accuracy. She will destroy you. Be honest about what is validated (the math) versus what is a hypothesis (the thresholds and burnout correlation).

### 4. Puneet Chabra -- Associate Director (Program Management & Product Delivery), PepsiCo

**Company:** PepsiCo India -- Lay's, Kurkure, Tropicana, Pepsi. Has manufacturing plants, supply chain, distribution networks. Using AI for digital twins in factories (Siemens/NVIDIA partnership). Mix of corporate HQ workers (Gurgaon) and plant workers across India. Thousands of employees.

**Role:** Tech/digital role within PepsiCo. Manages large-scale technology programs and delivers digital products internally. Business-meets-tech profile. Understands project execution, delivery timelines, scalability.

**Industry context:** PepsiCo's workforce is 70% non-digital -- factory workers, sales reps, distributors. The 30% at HQ use enterprise tools (SAP SuccessFactors, Workday). He lives at the intersection of technology and large-scale operations.

**What he will ask:** "How does this work for a workforce that is 70% non-digital? How does it integrate with SAP SuccessFactors? What is the implementation timeline for 10,000+ employees?"

**How to impress:** Show enterprise readiness. "We start with your HQ knowledge workers -- the 30% generating digital signals. Phase 2 integrates with SAP HCM shift and attendance data to extend coverage to plant managers and operational teams. Phased rollout, measurable results at each stage."

**What NOT to say:** Do not assume everyone at PepsiCo has a laptop. Acknowledge the blue-collar workforce directly. Do not say "Slack integration" first -- say "enterprise HRMS integration."

### 5. Akshay Kashyap -- VP (Data Engineering), NatWest Group

**Company:** NatWest (formerly Royal Bank of Scotland). India operations are a massive GCC with 19,000 employees across Gurgaon, Bengaluru, Chennai. Not a back-office -- an integrated global tech hub. Built their AI platform "Kepler" from India. Heavily regulated (GDPR, RBI).

**Role:** VP of Data Engineering. Deeply technical. Manages data infrastructure, pipelines, data platforms. Over a decade in data engineering across financial services. **This is the most technically rigorous judge on the panel.**

**Industry context:** Banking data is classified. Data residency, encryption, and compliance (GDPR + RBI) are non-negotiable. 19,000 knowledge workers generating rich digital signals -- the ideal Sentinel customer profile.

**What he will ask:** "Walk me through the data architecture. How does ingestion work? What is the two-vault schema at the database level? What is your precision/recall tradeoff? How do you handle GDPR data residency?"

**How to impress:** Go deep on architecture. "Ingestion hashes PII with HMAC-SHA256 at the edge before storage. Vault A holds only hashed IDs and behavioral events -- no foreign key to Vault B. Vault B stores AES-256 encrypted identity data. No JOIN is possible between schemas. Every identity resolution is audit-logged."

**What NOT to say:** Do not hand-wave on technical details. If you do not know, say "we have not built that yet" rather than bluffing. He will probe and he will know if you are making it up.

**Role:** LinkedIn Top AI Voice. Expertise in Strategy & Transformation, GenAI, Intelligent Automation, Process Mining. Previously Deputy Director of Process Excellence Transformation at PepsiCo (moved to A&M in January 2026). He has applied AI to process improvement at enterprise scale.

**Industry context:** Consulting, high-intensity, travel-heavy. He thinks in process maps, value streams, and measurable transformation outcomes. He has seen both corporate and consulting sides.

**What he will ask:** "What process does Sentinel actually automate? Where is the workflow? How do you measure transformation impact? Can A&M deploy this for clients in turnaround engagements?"

**How to impress:** Frame as a diagnostic tool that identifies ROOT CAUSES. "Sentinel does not just detect burnout symptoms -- it identifies which teams, processes, and workflows are generating burnout hotspots. In a turnaround scenario, this tells you where operational inefficiency is destroying your workforce before the P&L shows it."

**What NOT to say:** Do not position as "just an HR tool." He thinks in business transformation and measurable outcomes. HR tools are cost centers. Business intelligence tools get funded.

### 7. Danish Raza -- Advisor & Consultant, GOM & MITRA

**Company:** MITRA (Maharashtra Institution for Transformation) is a state government body modeled after NITI Aayog. Drives Maharashtra toward a $1 trillion economy through data-driven governance. GOM = Government of Maharashtra.

**Role:** Sector Expert at MITRA. Background is journalism and communications -- Hindustan Times (Assistant Editor), VICE (News Editor, South Asia), Firstpost, Alfred Friendly Press Fellow. Later moved into strategic communications for climate and policy organizations. Technical undergrad from NIT Bhopal + BARC.

**Industry context:** Government advisory and policy. He brings a STORYTELLING + GOVERNANCE + DATA perspective. Not deep tech. He is a communicator who asks "what is the human story?"

**What he will ask:** "Tell me the human story, not the technology. How transparent is this system? Can employees see what data is collected? What about government offices with no digital footprint?"

**How to impress:** Tell a narrative. "Imagine a state government department where attrition is 40%. HR runs an annual engagement survey -- 20% response rate, results arrive 3 months later. Meanwhile, three senior officers have already resigned. With Sentinel's metadata-only approach, the department head sees aggregate team health declining in real time and can intervene before the next resignation." Lead with the human problem, then show the tech.

**What NOT to say:** Do not lead with architecture diagrams. He is a storyteller. Speak in narratives, not schemas.

### 8. Vipin Kumar -- CIO (Global), UFLEX Ltd

**Company:** India's largest multinational flexible packaging company. Manufactures packaging films, printing cylinders, adhesives. Operations in India, UAE, Egypt, Poland, Mexico, Nigeria, USA. 6,000-10,000+ employees globally, mostly on production lines.

**Role:** Global CIO with 26+ years of IT experience. Oversees all technology across UFLEX's global operations. Focus: ERP/SAP implementation, factory automation, supply chain digitization, IoT on factory floors. Practical, implementation-focused. Not a research/strategy type.

**Industry context:** Traditional manufacturing. Most employees operate extruders and printing presses. They do not use laptops. Plants span countries with varying connectivity. Data sovereignty matters.

**What he will ask:** "My employees operate machines, not laptops. How does Sentinel work for them? What is actually built versus what is a slide deck? On-premise or cloud? What about data sovereignty across Nigeria, Egypt, Poland?"

**How to impress:** Be ruthlessly practical. "What works TODAY: metadata analysis for knowledge workers at your Noida HQ. What is on the roadmap: integration with SAP HCM and shift management systems for plant workers. We will not pretend to solve the factory floor on day one. We start with corporate, prove ROI, then extend." CIOs hate vaporware.

**What NOT to say:** Do not show a Slack dashboard as your opening slide. He has seen a hundred AI pitches. Show him what is real, running, and deployed.

### 9. Sandeep Pahuja -- AVP, TrucksUp

**Company:** Indian logistics tech startup focused on the trucking ecosystem. AI-powered load matching, fuel solutions, fleet management. About 150+ employees, growing 196% YoY. Tagline: "Ab apni chalao."

**Role:** AVP in a 150-person startup -- a senior leadership role. Likely manages teams across zones or functions. Deals with fleet operators, truck drivers, logistics managers daily. The workforce is blue-collar and field-based.

**Industry context:** In logistics, burnout literally kills people -- driver fatigue causes accidents. But drivers have zero digital desk footprint. TrucksUp is a startup, so enterprise SaaS pricing is out of reach.

**What he will ask:** "Our truck drivers are on the road 16 hours a day. Can Sentinel integrate with telematics? We are 150 people -- is this for us or only for 10,000-employee enterprises? What is the pricing?"

**How to impress:** Focus on retention ROI. "Every truck driver who quits costs you recruitment, training, and 2-3 weeks of an idle truck. Sentinel identifies which team managers are creating burnout hotspots in your operations team -- the people managing drivers. The $4/employee/month Starter tier works for a 150-person company."

**What NOT to say:** Do not use enterprise-only messaging. Show this works for mid-market companies too. Do not pretend you can track truck drivers -- be honest about the knowledge-worker scope and the HRMS integration roadmap.

### 11. Mudit Krishna -- Head of AI, JK Cement

**Company:** Major Indian cement manufacturer. Grey cement, white cement, wall putty, paints. Plants, kilns, quarries. 7,000-10,000+ employees, mostly at manufacturing sites. Corporate HQ in Delhi.

**Role:** Head of AI (appointed January 2026). Carnegie Mellon alumnus. Building JK Cement's AI strategy: scalable GenAI solutions, ML systems, enterprise analytics. Previously built RAG pipelines, LLM agents, predictive analytics for Fortune 500. **Deeply technical in AI/ML.**

**Industry context:** He is new to his role -- trying to prove AI value in a cement company. He appreciates practical AI applications over theoretical claims. His team works on production-grade ML systems, not research papers.

**What he will ask:** "What models do you use? Fine-tuned LLMs or classical ML? What is the RAG pipeline architecture? How do you handle cold-start? Show me precision, recall, and F1."

**How to impress:** Go deep on the AI stack. "Core detection is classical ML -- scipy.stats.linregress for velocity, scipy.stats.entropy for schedule chaos, networkx.betweenness_centrality for network analysis. These are deterministic, explainable. The LLM layer (Gemini 2.5 Flash) generates narratives and 1:1 agendas from math output only -- it never sees raw employee data. The 'Deterministic Sandwich' means decisions are math, text is AI."

**What NOT to say:** Do not say "we use AI" without showing the architecture. Surface-level AI claims mean nothing to a CMU-trained AI lead. Show the pipeline or do not mention it.

### 12. Arpit Rastogi -- CIO, Grant Thornton Bharat LLP

**Company:** One of India's largest professional services firms. Audit, tax, advisory, consulting, outsourcing. 8,000-9,000+ employees, predominantly knowledge workers (auditors, consultants, tax professionals). Part of the global Grant Thornton network.

**Role:** CIO and Executive Director. Won CIO100 Award 2025. Manages all internal technology for the firm. Thinks about productivity tools, cybersecurity, data management, collaboration platforms, AI adoption. Also considers how tools could be offered as advisory services to GT clients.

**Industry context:** Professional services. Consultants work on client sites with fragmented digital footprints across client environments. They context-switch across 3-4 clients. GT is also a potential reseller -- if they like Sentinel, they could recommend it to their own clients.

**What he will ask:** "Our consultants work on client sites -- fragmented digital footprint. How do you handle that? What is your security posture -- SOC 2? ISO 27001? Could we offer this as an advisory service to our clients?"

**How to impress:** Lead with security and the reseller angle. "SOC 2 aligned architecture. RBAC controls who sees what at every level. The partner opportunity: GT deploys Sentinel internally, validates results, then offers 'Workforce Health Advisory' as a service to clients. You become both user and channel partner."

**What NOT to say:** Do not ignore the sell-through opportunity. If GT can also recommend this to their clients, that is a massive incentive for him to champion the product.
